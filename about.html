All University Orchestra

The All University Orchestra was founded in 1997 by David Koes, with the aim of giving all students at Carnegie Mellon the opportunity to perform with a symphony orchestra.

This ensemble performs two concerts per academic year. Rehearsals take place on Sunday evenings, in the Connan Room located in the University Center; see the Rehearsals page for details.

If you are interested in joining the AUO, come to our first rehearsal which will be held Sunday, September 7th from 7:30 - 10:30 p.m. in the UC Connan room and bring your instrument. If you have any other questions, please email us at auo@andrew.cmu.edu . In addition, please take a few moments to fill out the Form of Existence and read the syllabus to become acquainted with some of our policies.

If you would like to help the AUO continue putting on free concerts, donate online - please see our Support page for details.

Syllabus

String Theory

String Theory is a companion ensemble to AUO's main orchestra. Founded in 2008 - by Maria Sensi- Sellner - as a traditional string ensemble, String Theory is now a small chamber orchestra, performing independent concerts. Additionally, the ensemble aims to present a wide range of repertoire at a high musical standard--from Corelli to Shostakovich--providing students the experience of playing in a smaller ensemble.

Rehearsals are Saturdays from 3:30 to 5:00 p.m. in Margaret Morrison Room 119. For more information, please email AUO Music Director Jeffrey Klefstad at klefstjd@gmail.com .

Flute Choir

Flute Choir is an independent chamber ensemble affiliated with the AUO. Comprised of both AUO members and outside CMU students this ensemble performs during the AUO Chamber concerts along side with String Theory. Traditional repertoire perform include original compositions and arrangements by CMU composition students as well as works found in the flute choir repertoire.
