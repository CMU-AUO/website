Welcome

Welcome to the website of Carnegie Mellon's All University Orchestra. The AUO is an organization composed of CMU undergrads, graduate students, staff, and faculty who love to make music. Everyone is welcome to participate! Please see the Auditions page for audition materials.

The AUO puts on one concert per semester, available free of charge to members of the CMU community and the public. See our Concerts page for this year's schedule and recordings of some of our past concerts.

2016-2017 Audition Sign-Ups

August 28, 2016

Dear Returning and Interested Members:

Welcome back! I am very excited to begin rehearsals in a couple of weeks!

We will be holding Informal Auditions (cannot stress that enough) on Thursday, September 8th and Friday, September 9th from 6:00-10:30pm in MM127. These auditions are for part assignments only, and EVERYONE is still welcome to join!

If either of these dates do not work or all of the audition time slots have been filled, please email me for an alternate time. Please Sign-up for only ONE time and do not sign-up for a time someone else has taken.

2016-2017 Season Announcement

July 17, 2016

The All University Orchestra at Carnegie Mellon University is excited to celebrate their 19th season 'Le Voci Del Destino with programs of powerful bravura!

VERDI & RACHMANINOFF

Sunday, December 4th – Carnegie Music Hall – 4:30 p.m.

Join us in December for a passionate program, including the overture from Verdi’s La Forza del Destino, Max Bruch’s soaring Violin Concerto No. 2 featuring AUO Concerto & Aria Competition winner Irene Sun, followed by Serge Rachmaninoff’s
fateful Symphony No. 2.


COPLAND & BEETHOVEN

Sunday, April 9th – Soldiers and Sailors Museum – 4:30 p.m.

Come hear the magnificent words of America’s 16th President through Aaron Copland’s Lincoln Portrait. Take in the virtuosic writing of Paganini’s Violin Concerto No. 1 featuring AUO Concerto & Aria Competition winner, Taisuke Yasuda. Finally, share in the celebration with Beethoven’s ‘Heroic’ Symphony No. 3.


AUO Chamber Music

Please be on the lookout for performances by our chamber ensembles, String Theroy and Flute Choir, throughout the season.


The AUO is funded by the Department of Student Activities. All concerts are free and open to the public.
